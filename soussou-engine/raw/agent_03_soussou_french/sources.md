## Language Background

### About Soussou (Susu/Soso)

- **Language Family**: Mande (Niger-Congo)
- **Closest Relative**: Yalunka
- **Speakers**: ~2.5-3 million
- **Primary Location**: Guinea (coastal/maritime region)
- **Also Spoken In**: Sierra Leone, Guinea-Bissau, Senegal
- **Official Status**: One of Guinea's national languages
- **Writing Systems**: Latin alphabet (primary), Arabic script, N'Ko script

### Linguistic Features

- **Word Order**: SOV (Subject-Object-Verb)
- **Tone**: Tonal language
- **Gender**: No grammatical gender
- **Articles**: No definite/indefinite articles
- **Plural**: Formed by adding '-e' to singular
- **Verb System**: 4 affixes (-ma, -xi, -fe, -∅) + 6 object markers

### French Loanwords

French loanwords are NORMAL in Soussou due to:
- French colonial history
- French as official language of Guinea
- Code-switching in urban areas

Common borrowed terms include technical vocabulary, days of week (via Arabic), and modern concepts. The MISSION document notes: "comprendfé", "woto", "bateau" are acceptable.
