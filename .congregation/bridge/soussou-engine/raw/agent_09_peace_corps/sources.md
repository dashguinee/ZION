# Peace Corps Susu Language Resources

## Summary

This collection contains **156 vocabulary entries** extracted from Peace Corps educational materials and related Susu language learning resources. The vocabulary covers numbers, days of the week, greetings, pronouns, family terms, common verbs, grammar particles, household items, and practical phrases.

## Language Notes

### Characteristics
- **Language Family**: Mande (Niger-Congo)
- **Closest Relative**: Yalunka
- **Grammar Type**: SOV (Subject-Object-Verb), suffixing
- **Noun Classes**: None
- **Gender**: None
- **Articles**: None (no definite/indefinite articles)
- **Negation**: Particle "mu"

### Identifying Features
- **Pronouns**: Ntan/Itan/Ana/Won/Wo/E
- **Possessives**: M'ma/Akha
- **Negation**: m'ma/mu

### French Loanwords
French loanwords are normal and accepted in Susu due to Guinea's colonial history. Examples include:
- tebili (table)
- fore (fork/chair from "fauteuil")
- kopi (cup from "coupe")
- maakiti (market from "marche")
