## Primary Academic Sources

### Journal Articles

1. **Shluinsky, Andrey (2014)**
   - "Verbal prefixes mà- and rà- in Susu and lexical features of verbal stems"
   - *Mandenkan*, Issue 52
   - URL: https://journals.openedition.org/mandenkan/329
   - PDF: https://llacan.cnrs.fr/PDF/Mandenkan52/52shluinsky.pdf
   - **Content**: Detailed analysis of Susu verbal prefixes, derivational morphology, argument structure changes

2. **Green, Christopher R., Anderson, Jonathan C., & Obeng, Samuel G. (2013)**
   - "Interacting tonal processes in Susu"
   - *Mandenkan*, Issue 50
   - URL: https://journals.openedition.org/mandenkan/509
   - **Content**: Phonological analysis of Susu tone system

### Historical Grammar References

3. **Duport, J.H.A. & Rawle, R. (1869)**
   - *Outlines of a grammar of the Susu language (West Africa)*
   - URL: https://theswissbay.ch/pdf/Books/Linguistics/Mega%20linguistics%20pack/African/Niger-Congo/Mande/Susu%20Language,%20Outlines%20of%20a%20Grammar%20of%20the%20(Duport)%20(1886).pdf
   - HathiTrust Catalog: https://catalog.hathitrust.org/Record/102710515

4. **Houis, Maurice (1963)**
   - *Étude descriptive de la langue Susu*
   - Institut Fondamental d'Afrique Noire, Université de Dakar
   - **Note**: Key foundational reference for Susu linguistics

5. **Brunton, H. (1802)**
   - *A grammar of the Susoo language*
   - Includes names of Susoo towns near Rio Pongas

## Gaps in Available Sources

The following areas had limited freely-available online documentation:

1. **Detailed verb conjugation paradigms** - Most sources describe the system generally without complete tables
2. **Complete vocabulary lists by semantic domain** - Scattered across multiple sources
3. **Dialectal variation** - Little documentation of regional differences
4. **Historical linguistics** - Limited comparative Mande data
5. **Modern corpus data** - No large text corpora identified
